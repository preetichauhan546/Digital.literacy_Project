&#x20;***CASE STUDY : CYBERBULLYING*** 



&#x20;***1. Introduction***

*Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where individuals are targeted through digital platforms such as social media, messaging apps, and online communities. It can include threats, insults, spreading rumors, or emotional manipulation.*



&#x20;***2. Real Case: Megan Meier Case***

*One of the most well-known cyberbullying incidents is the case of Megan Meier, a 13-year-old girl from Missouri, USA.*



&#x20;***3. How the Crime Happened***

*1. A fake profile was made and was named “Josh” on an app called MySpace.*

*2. By using the account, friendly relationship was made with Megan.*

*3. After gaining her trust, the messages suddenly turned cruel and hurtful.*

*4. She received emotionally damaging messages, including rejection and insults.*

*5. The harassment continued over time, deeply affecting her mental health.*



***4. Targeted Victims***

*Cyberbullying mainly targets:*

* *Teenagers and young social media users*  
* *Emotionally vulnerable individuals*  
* &#x20;*People who are highly active online*  



***5. Consequences***

*- Severe emotional distress and anxiety*  

*- Loss of self-confidence*  

*- Social withdrawal*  

*- In extreme cases, tragic outcomes like loss of life*  



***6. Conclusion***

*This case highlights the dangers of cyberbullying and the need for responsible online behavior. Awareness, parental guidance, and strict action against online harassment are essential to prevent such incidents. Users should report abusive behavior and avoid interacting with unknown or suspicious profiles.*



***7. Key Safety Tips***

*- Do not trust unknown online profiles*  

*- Never share personal information with strangers*  

*- Report and block abusive users*  

*- Talk to a trusted person if facing online harassment*
